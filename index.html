🏆서민호의 주기율표

The number of valence electrons in SN is 4, and tin is produced through the S-process of beta decay of radioactive isotopes of indium in stars between 0.6 and 10 solar masses. It is the 49th most abundant element in the Earth's crust, and is contained at a concentration of about 2 ppm. It is not found in its pure form in nature. Tinite (SnO2) is the main source of tin, the rest is mainly in the form of sulphides. It is mainly produced in China, Malaysia, Peru and Indonesia. Unfortunately, tin is not known who its first discoverer was. There are 10 stable isotopes of tin. Among the stable isotopes, 'tin-120' is the most abundant and 'tin-115' is the least. In addition, 29 unstable radioactive isotopes are known, all of which have half-lives of less than one year, except tin-126 (half-life of about 230,000 years).

원소의 특징으로는 주석은 공기 중이나 수중에서 안정하고 잘 부식되지 않으며 인체에도 해롭지 않습니다. 또 녹는점이 낮고 전성·연성이 커서 압연 (압연은 금속을 세공하는 과정에서 회전하는 두 원통 사이로 들어가게 만들어 재료의 모습을 변화시키는 공정입니다.) 하여 얇은 판을 만들기 쉽습니다. 이와 같은 성질 때문에 철·강철·구리 표면의 도금에 가장 많이 사용되는데요, 그중 철판 표면에 도금한 것을 양철이라 합니다. 주석의 영어는 tin이고 화학기호는 Sn이기에 기억하기 불편할 것입니다. 원소기호가 된 Sn은 라틴어 stannum (원소)에서 유래했으며, 영어 tin은 'Tinnia' (신의 이름)에서 왔습니다.
As a feature of the element, tin is stable in air or water, does not corrode well, and is not harmful to the human body. In addition, it has a low melting point and high malleability and ductility, so it is easy to make thin plates by rolling (rolling is a process of changing the shape of a material by making it fit between two rotating cylinders in the process of working metal). Because of these properties, it is most often used for plating the surface of iron, steel, and copper. Tin's English is tin, and its chemical symbol is Sn, so it will be inconvenient to remember. Sn, which became the element symbol, is derived from the Latin stannum (element), and the English tin is from 'Tinnia' (the name of God).

The most typical use of tin is solder used to connect metal tubes and circuits in electronic products as an alloy with lead. In addition, many tin alloys are used as metal materials for various purposes, such as bells, pipes of pipe organs, tableware and ornaments, and large bearings for heavy loads. The main ore minerals of tin are stannite and stannite, and tin deposits include quarry deposits, pegmatite deposits, hydrothermal deposits, and skarn deposits. Ore deposits and pegmatite deposits produce lithite, while hydrothermal deposits and skarn deposits produce statite and stanite. In particular, hydrothermal deposits sometimes produce tungsten, molybdenum, or silver minerals in addition to lithite.
